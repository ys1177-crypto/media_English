The headline says conflict is "driving" hunger. What does this word choice suggest? What do you think 'conflict' here refers to?
Sample answer: "Driving" suggests conflict is the active, primary force pushing hunger forward — not just one of many factors, but the engine. "Conflict" likely refers to multiple ongoing wars (Iran, Sudan, Gaza, Lebanon, etc.) rather than a single conflict.
Q2
Which countries or regions do you think are most affected by the hunger crisis? How might rich countries respond to this issue?
Sample answer: Countries most affected are likely conflict zones such as Sudan, Gaza, Afghanistan, and Somalia, as well as climate-vulnerable regions like the Sahel. Rich countries could respond by increasing aid funding, pressuring warring parties to allow humanitarian access, investing in sustainable agriculture in vulnerable regions, and strengthening international institutions like the WFP. However, the article suggests political will — not resources — is the main obstacle.

🌍 The World Food Programme (WFP)

유엔 산하 식량 지원 기구로, 120개국 이상에서 활동하는 세계 최대 인도주의 기구입니다. 2020년 노벨 평화상을 수상했습니다. 예산은 거의 전액 정부·민간의 자발적 기부로 운영되며, 미국이 역사적으로 전체 예산의 약 1/3~40%를 부담해 왔습니다.

✂️ USAID & The US Aid Cuts

USAID는 미국의 대외 원조를 담당하는 정부 기관입니다. 트럼프 2기 행정부 출범 이후 대규모 예산 삭감이 이루어졌으며, WFP에 대한 미국의 지원금은 약 2/3(30억 달러) 삭감되었습니다.

⚖️ International Humanitarian Law

국제 인도주의법(IHL)은 무력 충돌 시 민간인·구호 요원·전쟁 포로를 보호하는 규범으로, 제네바 협약이 핵심 법적 틀입니다. 최근 IHL 위반이 증가하면서 인도주의 업무가 매우 위험해지고 있습니다.

🌿 Fertiliser Prices & Food Security

질소 비료는 천연가스로 만들기 때문에 에너지 가격과 직결됩니다. 전쟁이 석유·가스 공급을 교란하면 비료값이 올라 식량 생산이 감소하고 식품 가격이 폭등합니다. 저소득국 사람들은 소득의 80~90%를 식비에 쓰기 때문에 이 충격에 특히 취약합니다.

Section 1 · Paragraphs 1–3 · Introduction & Scale of the Crisis
Geopolitics & Humanitarian Crisis · Financial Times

How Conflict Is Driving Hunger

The Swede who runs the World Food Programme on its hardest year for decades

Carl Skau, the Swede who currently runs the World Food Programme, rushed back from a visit to Ukraine's front lines when war broke out in Iran. He knew the new conflict would worsen the largely overlooked global hunger crisis. Rising oil prices, he explains, "always" raise food prices. Some people in poor countries "spend 80 to 90 per cent of their disposable income on food" (yet get less attention than American motorists paying more for petrol). Hoping to extract funds from a cash-strapped French government, Skau dropped by Paris, where he met the local Anglo-American Press Association in a café. Sitting beside a blackboard displaying delicious dishes, he recounted how conflict is driving hunger.

Skau had called 2025 "probably the hardest year" for hunger in decades. For the first time this century, the WFP, the world's largest humanitarian organisation, declared two simultaneous famines, in the war zones of Gaza and Sudan. Gaza has since exited famine — though "it's hard to describe how destroyed it is", notes Skau, a frequent visitor — while Sudan's plight has worsened. "Every month we reach five million people in Sudan. It's the hardest place to work," he says.

Now, with new wars, and fertiliser prices soaring, the estimated total of 318 million people worldwide facing food insecurity could jump to a record, projects the WFP. "I think 70 to 80 per cent is conflict driven," says Skau.

Section 2 · Paragraphs 4–5 · The Economics of Aid & Donor Cuts

Stopping starvation pays off. The World Bank estimates that $1 spent addressing undernutrition generates an expected return of $23, partly because stunted children rarely live productive lives. It's also affordable. A six-week treatment of high-calorie peanut paste costing less than $45 can save a child. But it's too much for rich countries. The US, the WFP's biggest donor, has cut funding by about two-thirds, or $3bn. Meanwhile Donald Trump is seeking another $200bn to fight his war with Iran. After Elon Musk boasted about "feeding USAID into the woodchipper", a paper in The Lancet projected more than 14 million additional deaths from those cuts by 2030. One measure of each US president is his global death toll. The often destructive George W Bush also saved an estimated 25 million lives, largely by funding HIV treatment in poor countries. Trump decimated the programme.

"It's not only the US," notes Skau. The UK, Germany and his native Sweden have slashed aid too. Gulf funders are particularly "transactional", he remarks. Emerging economies including China barely fund the WFP. Nor does the private sector. Skau says, "I was at the AI summit in India. There are certainly a lot of people with a lot of money. But it's not coming forward." His comment, "It's a bit unfortunate, isn't it?"

"feeding USAID into the woodchipper"

"projected more than 14 million additional deaths"
additional = 기존에 발생할 사망자 외에 '추가로' 발생할 사망 → 삭감이 없었다면 살 수 있었던 사람들의 수입니다.

Sentence G — Implied Argument
"He believes the world 'kind of should be agreeing' that children shouldn't die. But absent that agreement, he appeals to rich countries' self-interest: 'A hungry world is an unstable world.'"
absent that agreement 전치사/분사구 (= in the absence of)   he 주어   appeals to 동사구   rich countries' self-interest   : "A hungry world is an unstable world." 콜론: 구체적 내용 제시
"absent that agreement" — absent가 전치사처럼 쓰여 "그 합의가 없는 상태에서"를 의미합니다. 격식체·법률·외교 문서에서 자주 씁니다.
appeals to self-interest = 도덕적 호소 대신 이해타산에 호소하다.
🇰🇷 수사학: 도덕적 호소 vs 이해타산 호소

스카우는 두 단계로 주장합니다:

1단계 (도덕적 호소): "kind of should be agreeing that children shouldn't die"
→ "kind of"는 헤징(hedging) — 직접 요구하지 않고 완곡하게 표현합니다.

2단계 (이해타산 호소): "A hungry world is an unstable world."
→ 도덕적 합의가 없더라도, 기아는 결국 부유한 나라에도 불안정을 가져온다는 현실적 논거입니다.

"absent that agreement" = "그 합의가 없다면"
격식체 표현: in the absence of that agreement = absent that agreement
Sentence H
"Skau says that with 'big powers' challenging international humanitarian law, government officials in war zones feel emboldened to do likewise."
Skau 주어   says 동사   that … 명사절 (목적어) 안에:
    with 'big powers' challenging international humanitarian law with+O+분사 (동시 상황)   government officials in war zones 명사절 주어   feel emboldened 동사구   to do likewise to부정사 (부연)
with + O + 현재분사: "O가 ~하는 상황에서" — big powers가 능동적으로 IHL에 도전하므로 현재분사(challenging) 사용.
emboldened = feel emboldened to do = ~할 용기를 얻다.
do likewise = 마찬가지로 하다 (= do the same).
🇰🇷 "with + O + 분사" 구문:

with 'big powers' challenging international humanitarian law
→ "강대국들이 국제 인도주의법에 도전하는 상황에서"

현재분사 vs 과거분사 선택:
✅ with big powers challenging → 능동 → 현재분사
✅ with the law being challenged → 수동 → 과거분사
