Why Understudies Are Seeing Their Wages Rise

The Economist · Feb 19, 2026 · 단락별 읽기 + 문장 분석 + 문제
Section 1 · P1–P2 · Hook + What is an understudy?

"The bedrock of Broadway": that is how Hugh Jackman, an Australian film star, described understudies during a curtain call of "The Music Man" in 2021. Beside him stood Kathy Voytko, an understudy who had stepped in to play the leading lady with a few hours' notice and one hasty rehearsal. Rewarded with a standing ovation from the audience, she cried.

It was a reminder of just how reliant Broadway and West End productions are on understudies, the invisible men and women of the theatre world. These stand-ins come in many different guises. For a play, they are often the familiar figure you might picture: a talented whippersnapper waiting in the dressing room for their chance to dazzle (and suppressing temptations to spike the star's drink). For a musical, understudies are usually part of the all-singing, all-dancing ensemble who have learned the lead's parts, too. If they are called upon, there are "swings", understudies' understudies, who fill in. Swings regularly cover up to ten different roles; some cover as many as 20.

"These stand-ins come in many different guises. For a play, they are often the familiar figure you might picture: a talented whippersnapper waiting in the dressing room for their chance to dazzle (and suppressing temptations to spike the star's drink)."
Subject
These stand-ins
"stand-ins" = understudies. "These" refers back to the understudies just mentioned.
Main verb
come in many different guises
"guises" = 모습, 형태. Idiomatic: "come in different guises" = appear in different forms.
Modifier (colon)
a talented whippersnapper waiting in the dressing room for their chance to dazzle
"whippersnapper" = 패기 넘치는 신참, 젊은이 (slightly humorous, old-fashioned). "dazzle" = 눈부시게 하다. The colon introduces an example of one type of understudy.
Aside (brackets)
(and suppressing temptations to spike the star's drink)
This is dry wit — the writer jokes that understudies might be tempted to poison the lead so they get their chance onstage. It humanises the frustration of the role while making the reader smile. "spike" here = secretly add poison/drugs to a drink.
"and suppressing temptations to spike the star's drink" — 이 괄호 안 표현은 The Economist 특유의 건조한 유머(dry wit)입니다. 언더스터디가 주연 배우의 음료에 독을 넣고 싶은 충동을 억누른다는 농담인데, 이를 통해 언더스터디의 심리적 좌절감(무대에 서고 싶지만 기다려야만 하는 상황)을 재치 있게 표현합니다. 겉으로는 웃기지만, 실제로는 그들의 직업이 얼마나 불안하고 인내가 필요한지를 드러냅니다.
"It was a reminder of just how reliant Broadway and West End productions are on understudies, the invisible men and women of the theatre world."
Subject
It
"It" refers back to the whole scene described in P1 — Jackman's tribute and Voytko's ovation.
Verb + complement
was a reminder of just how reliant … are on understudies
"just how reliant … are on" = 얼마나 의존하는지. Notice the inverted word order inside the "how" clause: "how reliant [they] are" not "how they are reliant." This is a degree clause (정도절).
Appositive

This phrase renames "understudies" — it adds description without a new clause. "Invisible" is ironic: they do essential work but nobody knows their names.
"Beside him stood Kathy Voytko, an understudy who had stepped in to play the leading lady with a few hours' notice and one hasty rehearsal."
Fronted prepositional phrase (도치)
Beside him
정상 어순이라면 "Kathy Voytko stood beside him"이지만, "Beside him"을 문장 앞으로 꺼내면서 주어와 동사가 도치됩니다 → "Beside him stood Kathy Voytko." 이런 도치(inversion)는 문학적이고 극적인 효과를 주며, 위치나 상황을 먼저 강조할 때 씁니다. Hugh Jackman 옆에 누가 서 있는지에 독자의 시선을 집중시킵니다.
Inverted verb + subject
stood Kathy Voytko
도치된 구조: 동사(stood)가 주어(Kathy Voytko)보다 먼저 옵니다. 일반 어순: "Kathy Voytko stood beside him." 도치 어순: "Beside him stood Kathy Voytko." 비교: "In the corner sat an old man." / "On the stage stood the star."
Appositive + relative clause
an understudy who had stepped in to play the leading lady
"an understudy" = Kathy Voytko를 부연 설명하는 동격어구. "who had stepped in" = 관계절 (had stepped = 과거완료, 공연보다 더 이전에 일어난 일을 표시). "step in" = 대타로 들어가다. "to play" = 목적을 나타내는 부정사.
With-phrase (조건/상황)
with a few hours' notice and one hasty rehearsal
"with + 명사" 구문으로 상황이나 조건을 나타냅니다. "a few hours' notice" = 몇 시간 전에 통보받음 ("notice" 앞의 소유격 어포스트로피에 주목). "hasty" = 급하게 이루어진. 이 짧은 with-구 하나로 상황의 긴박함을 압축적으로 전달합니다.
도치(Inversion) 정리:
전치사구(Beside him)가 문장 앞으로 나오면 주어와 동사의 순서가 바뀝니다.

일반 어순: Kathy Voytko stood beside him.
도치 어순: Beside him stood Kathy Voytko.

이런 도치는 소설, 신문 기사, 격식체 글쓰기에서 자주 사용됩니다. 위치나 배경을 먼저 제시하고 인물을 뒤에 등장시키는 극적 효과가 있습니다.
"Rewarded with a standing ovation from the audience, she cried."
Fronted participial phrase (분사구문)
Rewarded with a standing ovation from the audience
"Rewarded"는 과거분사(past participle)로 시작하는 분사구문(participial phrase)입니다. 주절의 주어(she)가 reward를 받은 것 — 즉 수동의 의미입니다. 완전한 절로 풀면: "Because she was rewarded with a standing ovation from the audience, she cried." 분사구문은 이 인과 관계를 훨씬 간결하게 표현합니다.
Main clause
she cried
단 두 단어로 이루어진 주절입니다. 앞의 긴 분사구문과 대비되어 감정적 충격이 극대화됩니다.
분사구문(Participial phrase) 정리:

분사구문은 접속사 + 절을 분사(현재분사/과거분사) + 구로 압축한 것입니다.

완전한 절: Because she was rewarded with a standing ovation, she cried.
분사구문: Rewarded with a standing ovation, she cried.

"Rewarded"가 과거분사인 이유: she가 reward를 받은 것(수동)이기 때문입니다. 만약 능동의 의미라면 현재분사를 씁니다.
예) Receiving a standing ovation, she cried. (그녀가 박수를 받으면서 울었다)

분사구문을 문장 앞에 배치하면 → 원인/상황을 먼저 제시하고 결과로 이어지는 극적 흐름을 만듭니다.

Section 2 · P3 · The economics — insurance policy

Understudies make up about a quarter of a big show's cast. In some cases, there are as many performers offstage as on it. But despite weeks of rehearsals and pay, understudies may never see action. They are considered an insurance policy for a theatre producer hedging against the risk of a show being cancelled. Refunding just one night of "The Lion King" on the West End or Broadway could cost up to £200,000 ($275,000) in lost revenue. When you consider that fees for an understudy covering two roles are around £1,000 a week in London, or $2,900 in New York, it is a small price to pay for "hakuna matata".

Sentence Clinic — 문장 해부
"They are considered an insurance policy for a theatre producer hedging against the risk of a show being cancelled."
Subject
They
= understudies
Passive verb
are considered
Passive voice: the agent (who considers them?) is omitted — this is deliberate, suggesting it is a general industry understanding.
Complement
an insurance policy
This is the central metaphor of the whole article. The sub-headline ("Understudies are an insurance policy") introduces it; the article builds on it through "premiums", "hedging" etc.
Modifier (participial phrase)
for a theatre producer hedging against the risk of a show being cancelled
"hedging" = 위험을 분산하다 (finance term). "hedging against" = taking precautions against. "a show being cancelled" = gerund phrase acting as the object of "risk".
"hedging against the risk of a show being cancelled" — "hedge"는 금융 용어로 '위험을 분산하다'는 뜻입니다. 여기서 "a show being cancelled"는 동명사 구문으로, "risk of"의 목적어 역할을 합니다. 전체 구조: [the risk] [of] [a show being cancelled] = 공연이 취소될 위험.
"When you consider that fees for an understudy covering two roles are around £1,000 a week in London, or $2,900 in New York, it is a small price to pay for 'hakuna matata'."
Adverbial clause
When you consider that fees … are around £1,000 a week in London, or $2,900 in New York
"When you consider that …" = 고려하면, 감안하면. This is an adverbial clause of condition/concession, setting up the comparison that follows.
Main clause
it is a small price to pay
"it" = the understudy's weekly fee. "a small price to pay" = 치러야 할 작은 대가 — comparing the weekly fee to the £200,000 cancellation cost.
Dry wit (allusion)
for "hakuna matata"
"Hakuna matata" is Swahili for "no worries" — the famous song from The Lion King. The writer chose this phrase because the paragraph is about The Lion King's cancellation costs. It is ironic and playful: the "no worries" phrase represents the peace of mind that understudies provide.
"hakuna matata"(하쿠나 마타타)는 스와힐리어로 "걱정 없다"는 뜻으로, 바로 앞에서 언급된 뮤지컬 '라이온 킹'의 유명한 노래입니다. 작가가 이 단어를 선택한 이유: ① 바로 앞 문장에서 라이온 킹을 예시로 들었기 때문에 자연스럽게 연결됨 ② "걱정 없다" = 언더스터디 덕분에 프로듀서가 공연 취소 걱정을 덜 수 있다는 의미 ③ The Economist 특유의 건조한 유머.

Section 3 · P4–P5 · Wages rising + Why?

Understudies' rates are rising faster than those of standard performers. In October Actors' Equity, an American union, announced its latest agreement with Broadway producers, providing a 3% increase for the base rate paid to all actors and a 30% increase for additional swing responsibilities. Swings now earn an extra 6% over a standard performer on the minimum pay rate. In Britain they earn nearly 15% more. Equity, the British performing-arts union, is seeking an increase of around 50% for those additional responsibilities across their main collective agreements.

Why are understudies coming out on top? "It is an incredibly difficult job," says Charlotte Bence of Equity. "We want that essential contribution to be properly recognised." Learning the vocals, moves and lines of up to ten actors — some of which might be lead roles — is no easy feat. They have less rehearsal time, so their memories must be better and vocal ranges wider, all the while being ready to perform a role with little warning.

Sentence Clinic — 문장 해부
"Learning the vocals, moves and lines of up to ten actors — some of which might be lead roles — is no easy feat."
Subject (gerund phrase)
Learning the vocals, moves and lines of up to ten actors
"Learning …" is a gerund phrase (동명사구) acting as the subject of the sentence. Three things are listed in parallel: vocals / moves / lines.
Dash insertion
— some of which might be lead roles —
"some of which" = 그 중 일부는 (non-defining relative clause). "which" refers to "up to ten actors' roles". The dashes show this is additional, interruptive information — not essential to the grammar, but important to the argument.
대시(—) 사이의 "some of which might be lead roles"는 비제한적 관계절(non-defining relative clause)입니다. "which"는 앞의 "ten actors의 역할들"을 가리킵니다. 대시를 사용한 이유: 쉼표나 괄호 대신 대시를 쓰면 독자의 시선이 더 강하게 멈추고, 그 정보가 더 강조됩니다. 이 삽입구는 언더스터디의 부담이 단순한 조역이 아니라 주연 역할까지 포함한다는 핵심 정보를 전달합니다.
Main verb + complement
is no easy feat
"no easy feat" = 쉬운 일이 아니다 (idiomatic understatement). "feat" = 위업, 묘기. The whole gerund phrase "Learning …" is the subject — that is why the verb is singular "is" not "are".
"They have less rehearsal time, so their memories must be better and vocal ranges wider, all the while being ready to perform a role with little warning."
Clause 1
They have less rehearsal time
= understudies. Simple cause clause.
Connector
so
Introduces the result/consequence.
Clause 2 (ellipsis)
their memories must be better and vocal ranges [must be] wider
Notice "must be" appears only once but applies to both "memories" and "vocal ranges". This is ellipsis (생략) — the second "must be" is dropped to avoid repetition. Full version: "their memories must be better and their vocal ranges must be wider."
"memories must be better and vocal ranges wider" — 이 문장에서 "must be"가 두 번째로는 생략되어 있습니다. 이를 '생략(ellipsis)'이라고 합니다. 완전한 문장은 "their memories must be better and their vocal ranges must be wider"이지만, 반복을 피하기 위해 두 번째 "must be"와 "their"을 삭제했습니다. 이런 생략은 문장을 더 간결하고 리듬감 있게 만들어줍니다.
Participial phrase
all the while being ready to perform a role with little warning
"all the while" = 그러는 내내, 항상. "being ready … with little warning" = 예고 없이 갑자기 공연할 준비가 돼 있어야 하는. This participial phrase adds a simultaneous condition — they must do all this AND be ready at any moment.

Section 4 · P6–P8 · Social media · Podcast · Conclusion

Long accustomed to waiting in the wings, understudies are now being showered with more attention. One social-media account celebrates the West End debuts of understudies. A video of Hannah Lowther in an emergency rehearsal just four hours before a performance of "Heathers" — to cover a leading role she was not officially an understudy for — garnered nearly 60m views and made her a TikTok star.

"You're On Tonight", a new podcast, offers understudies a chance to soliloquise about their unique job. One episode features David Tarkenter, who was the cover for a one-man show starring and directed by Ralph Fiennes. "You're not paid to go on," he has said. "You're paid to be ready to go on." (He never went on.) Another features Clive Marlowe, who is the longest-serving understudy of all time, having been caught up in Agatha Christie's "The Mousetrap" for 15 years. (He has performed 75 times, about five times per year.)

Theatre's bedrock deserves more recognition. For lots of theatregoers, a blockbuster star is the draw. Refunds are sometimes demanded if an understudy plays the lead. But that attitude is short-sighted. Shirley MacLaine and Catherine Zeta-Jones, two Oscar-winning actresses, got their breaks as understudies. Ms Zeta-Jones was performing in "42nd Street" — a show that, aptly enough, follows an understudy's hit turn. Given the opportunity, understudies can go above and beyond.

Sentence Clinic — 문장 해부
"A video of Hannah Lowther in an emergency rehearsal just four hours before a performance of 'Heathers' — to cover a leading role she was not officially an understudy for — garnered nearly 60m views and made her a TikTok star."
Subject (long noun phrase)
A video of Hannah Lowther in an emergency rehearsal just four hours before a performance of "Heathers"
The subject is "A video" — everything that follows until the dash is modifying this noun. Notice how much information is packed into the subject before the verb appears.
Dash insertion (relative clause)
— to cover a leading role she was not officially an understudy for —
"to cover" = infinitive of purpose (목적을 나타내는 부정사). "she was not officially an understudy for" = contact clause (관계대명사 생략). Full form: "a leading role [that/which] she was not officially an understudy for." The dashes add dramatic emphasis — this detail is the surprising, story-making fact.
이 대시 삽입구 "to cover a leading role she was not officially an understudy for"에는 두 가지 문법 포인트가 있습니다.

① "to cover" = 목적을 나타내는 부정사 (= in order to cover)
② "she was not officially an understudy for" = 관계대명사가 생략된 접촉절 (contact clause). 완전한 형태는 "a leading role [which/that] she was not officially an understudy for". 마지막 "for"가 전치사로 문장 끝에 남아있는 구조입니다. 이것이 기사에서 가장 흥미로운 부분 — 그녀는 공식적으로 지정된 언더스터디도 아니었다는 것.
Main verbs
garnered nearly 60m views and made her a TikTok star
"garnered" = 얻다, 모으다 (more literary than "got"). Two parallel verbs: garnered [views] and made [her a TikTok star] — both results of the video.
"Ms Zeta-Jones was performing in '42nd Street' — a show that, aptly enough, follows an understudy's hit turn."
Subject + verb
Ms Zeta-Jones was performing in "42nd Street"
Past continuous (과거진행형) — emphasises that this was her ongoing situation at the time she got her break.
Appositive + relative clause
— a show that, aptly enough, follows an understudy's hit turn
"aptly enough" = 적절하게도 (parenthetical adverb). "aptly" signals that the writer is pointing out a pleasing coincidence — the show she was understudying in is literally about an understudy's breakthrough. "hit turn" = 성공적인 무대 기회.
"aptly enough" — 이 삽입 부사구는 The Economist 스타일의 dry wit(건조한 위트)입니다. 작가는 캐서린 제타 존스가 '42nd Street'에서 언더스터디를 하다가 스타가 됐는데, 그 뮤지컬 자체의 스토리가 언더스터디의 성공에 관한 것이라는 우연의 일치를 "aptly enough"(적절하게도)라는 표현으로 짚어냅니다.
